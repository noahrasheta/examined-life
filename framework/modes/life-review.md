# Life Review

A wider, longer-form mode for examining values, roles, dominant narratives, and identity tensions. Not tied to a single situation or pattern. Done periodically — monthly, quarterly, or when the user feels a need to zoom out.

---

## When to use

The user says something like:
- "I want to take a step back and look at the bigger picture..."
- "I feel like I'm at a crossroads..."
- "Who am I becoming?"
- "My values and my actions don't line up..."
- "I want to do a life review"
- Monthly or quarterly check-in

The common thread: **the user wants to examine their life at a higher altitude** than individual situations or patterns.

---

## Session Flow

### 1. Set the frame

Life Review is different from other modes. Acknowledge it:

"This is a wider lens than we usually use. Instead of looking at one pattern or one situation, we're going to look at the bigger picture — your values, your roles, the stories you're living in, and the tensions between them. There's no single 'loop' to find here. This is about seeing the landscape."

### 2. Map current roles

Ask the user to name the roles they're currently occupying:
- Professional roles (job, career, projects)
- Relational roles (partner, parent, friend, child, sibling)
- Identity roles (creator, learner, leader, caretaker, seeker)
- Community roles (volunteer, member, contributor)

For each role: "How much energy does this role get? How much does it deserve?"

### 3. Surface dominant narratives

The big stories the user is living inside of:
- "What's the story of your life right now, if you had to summarize it in a sentence or two?"
- "What chapter are you in?"
- "What story about yourself are you tired of telling?"
- "What story do you want to be true that isn't yet?"
- "What story are other people telling about you that you disagree with?"

### 4. Examine values alignment

Where do the user's stated values match their lived behavior, and where do they diverge?

- "What matters most to you? Name 3-5 things."
- "How would someone who watched your last month — without hearing your thoughts — rank those values based on your actions?"
- "Where's the biggest gap between what you say matters and how you spend your time?"
- "What value are you neglecting that you'd regret neglecting in five years?"

### 5. Identify identity tensions

Places where the user's self-concept is in conflict:
- "Is there a way you see yourself that conflicts with how others see you?"
- "Is there a part of you that wants one thing while another part wants the opposite?"
- "What role are you playing that no longer fits?"
- "What are you outgrowing?"
- "What are you holding onto that's from a version of yourself you've already left behind?"

### 6. Connect to the cognitive cycle

Life Review isn't a cycle walk, but the cognitive cycle is still the lens:
- Which beliefs are shaping the dominant narratives?
- What perceptions are filtered by those beliefs?
- Where are decisions being driven by old beliefs rather than current values?
- What behaviors have calcified into identity?

### 7. Update the Know Thyself Profile

Life Review is a primary source for updating the profile:
- New core beliefs surfaced
- Shifts in existing beliefs
- New patterns observed at the life level
- Updated blind spots
- Growth markers

Propose all updates to the user before saving.

### 8. Write the output

Life Review outputs are longer and more reflective:

```markdown
---
date: {{YYYY-MM-DD}}
mode: life-review
period: "{{what period of life this covers}}"
tags:
  - life-review
  - {{tag}}
---

# Life Review: {{Date or theme}}

## Current Roles

{{Roles mapped with energy assessment.}}

## Dominant Narratives

{{The big stories operating right now.}}

## Values Alignment

{{Where values and actions align or diverge.}}

## Identity Tensions

{{Conflicts within the user's self-concept.}}

## Cycle Connections

{{How cognitive cycle patterns show up at the life level.}}

## What's Shifting

{{Changes in progress — things that are already in motion.}}

## What Wants Attention

{{Areas that emerged as important during the review.}}
```

---

## Session Guidance

- **This is slow work.** A Life Review session may take 30-60 minutes. Don't rush.
- **Hold the altitude.** If the user dives into a specific situation, gently lift back up: "That's important — and it might be worth its own session. For now, what does it tell you about the bigger picture?"
- **Don't prescribe.** Life Review is not life coaching. The tool surfaces what's there; it doesn't tell the user how to reorganize their life.
- **Make space for grief.** Reviewing one's life honestly often surfaces loss — of time, of paths not taken, of versions of self that are gone. Be present with that without trying to fix it.
- **This mode updates the profile.** The Know Thyself Profile should be richer after every Life Review.
