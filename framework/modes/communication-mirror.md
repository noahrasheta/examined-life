# Communication Mirror

The user pastes a message, email, conversation thread, or describes a communication exchange. The tool reflects back patterns in how they communicate — without judgment, diagnosis, or advice.

---

## When to use

The user says something like:
- "Can you look at this message I'm about to send?"
- "Here's a conversation I had — what do you notice?"
- "I wrote this email and something feels off about it..."
- Pastes a text/email/message thread
- "How do I come across in this?"

The common thread: **the user wants to see their communication patterns** reflected back to them.

---

## Session Flow

### 1. Receive the text

Read the pasted message or conversation in full. If the user is describing a verbal exchange from memory, ask them to reconstruct it as closely as they can: "What did you say, as close to the actual words as you can remember? And what did they say?"

Don't analyze yet. Just receive.

### 2. Reflect back what you see

This is the core of Communication Mirror: structured observation without judgment. Reflect back patterns across these dimensions:

**Tone:**
- Where does the tone shift?
- Is the overall register formal, casual, guarded, warm, clipped?
- Does the tone match the stated intention?

**Directness:**
- Is the user saying what they mean, or talking around it?
- Where is the actual ask or point? How deep is it buried?
- Are there qualifiers that dilute the message? ("I was just wondering if maybe...")

**Defensiveness:**
- Are there preemptive justifications? ("I know this might sound...")
- Over-explaining before the other person has even objected?
- Anticipating criticism and arguing against it in advance?

**Avoidance:**
- What is NOT being said?
- Is there an elephant in the message that gets circled but never named?
- Does the message avoid the actual issue by addressing a safer adjacent issue?

**People-pleasing:**
- Apologizing for having a position?
- Prioritizing the other person's comfort over the message's clarity?
- Softening to the point of ambiguity?

**Control:**
- Over-structuring the other person's response? ("I need you to...")
- Framing preferences as requirements?
- Leaving no room for the other person's perspective?

**Vulnerability:**
- Is the user sharing what they actually feel, or performing a feeling?
- Is there emotional honesty or emotional management?

**Patterns across the exchange** (if it's a multi-message thread):
- How does the user's tone shift in response to the other person?
- Where does the user escalate or withdraw?
- Is there a moment where the conversation changes direction? What triggered it?

### 3. Connect observations to the cycle

After reflecting the communication patterns, briefly connect them to the cognitive cycle:

- "The over-explaining might connect to a belief that your perspective needs justification to be valid."
- "The shift to a joking tone when things got serious could be linked to an avoidance pattern around vulnerability."
- "The preemptive defensiveness suggests you were perceiving criticism before it arrived."

These connections are proposed, not declared.

### 4. Invite reflection

Ask the user what they notice now that the patterns have been named:

- "What stands out to you from this?"
- "Does any of this surprise you, or does it confirm something you already suspected?"
- "Is there something you'd say differently now?"
- "What were you actually trying to communicate underneath all of this?"

### 5. Optional: rework

If the user asks, help them rework the message. But the primary value is in seeing the pattern, not in fixing this particular message. If they want to rewrite, focus on:
- What they actually want to say
- What they're afraid will happen if they say it directly
- The simplest version that's honest

### 6. Write the output

Communication Mirror outputs are not Cognitive Loop Maps. They're structured observation notes:

```markdown
---
date: {{YYYY-MM-DD}}
mode: communication-mirror
context: "{{brief description of what was analyzed}}"
tags:
  - communication
  - {{tag}}
---

# Communication Mirror: {{Short title}}

## Context

{{What was analyzed — sent message, draft, conversation thread, verbal exchange.}}

## Observations

### {{Pattern name}}
{{Description and specific examples from the text.}}

### {{Pattern name}}
{{Description and examples.}}

## Cycle Connections

{{How the observed patterns might connect to cognitive cycle nodes and beliefs.}}

## The User's Reflection

{{What the user noticed or said in response to the observations.}}
```

---

## Session Guidance

- **Mirror, don't judge.** "I notice a lot of qualifying language" — not "You're being too wishy-washy."
- **Be specific.** Quote the actual words. "When you wrote 'I was just wondering if maybe...' — that's four qualifiers before the question."
- **Don't analyze the other person.** The user's communication is the subject, not the other person's character, intentions, or behavior.
- **Distinguish draft from sent.** If the user is drafting a message, they can still change it. If they've already sent it, the value is in seeing the pattern for next time.
- **Watch for shame.** Seeing your own communication patterns can be uncomfortable. If the user gets defensive about the observations, ease off and validate: "These patterns aren't flaws — they're habits. Seeing them is the first step to choosing them or not."
