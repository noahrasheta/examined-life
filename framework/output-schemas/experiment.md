## Design Principles

- **Small.** The experiment should be completable in a single instance. "Next time your partner says X, try Y" — not "Change how you communicate for a month."
- **Concrete.** The user should know exactly what to do. Not "Be more open" but "When you notice the urge to say 'I'm fine,' try saying what you're actually feeling instead."
- **Observable.** There should be something to notice — a reaction, a result, a feeling. The experiment produces data, not just effort.
- **Non-prescriptive.** The experiment tests a belief; it doesn't assume the belief is wrong. "Try this and see what happens" — not "Do this because your belief is wrong."
- **Low-stakes.** The cost of the experiment failing should be negligible. Don't propose experiments that could damage relationships or create crises.
- **Voluntary.** The user always chooses whether to try the experiment. It's a suggestion, not an assignment.
